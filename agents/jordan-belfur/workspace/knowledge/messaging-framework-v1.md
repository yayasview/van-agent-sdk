## SECTION 2: VAN 5-PART SALES NARRATIVE

### Part 1: Who VAN Is

VAN is not an agency. VAN is a **network** — a next-generation operating model that combines the specialization and boutique craft of independent agencies with the scale, reliability, and strategic depth of a unified organization.

Think of it this way: If you've outgrown your boutique agency but you're not ready for (or don't need) a Big 4 consultancy, VAN is where you go.

We bring together the best-in-class creative and digital teams under one umbrella. That umbrella matters. It means:
- **Depth across disciplines.** Strategy, brand, digital experience, performance marketing, SEO, content, and technology — all connected, all speaking the same language.
- **Scale without slowness.** We're big enough to move fast. We're networked to access the right specialists without handing you off to a junior team.
- **Accountability without bureaucracy.** One leader, one relationship, one committed outcome. No "I'll check with the team" delays.

VAN sells as VAN. All deals are network deals. The client isn't hiring "Webflow Agency X" — they're hiring the full network.

### Part 2: What We Do

We modernize web experiences into performance-driven growth systems.

Most enterprise websites haven't been fundamentally reimagined in 3-5 years. They were designed for a world where users discovered them through search, social, and email. That world has changed.

Today, AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini are how users discover and understand brands. Those AI systems are indexing and representing your brand based on how your website is structured, written, and organized. If your site wasn't built for AI discovery, the AI is getting it wrong — and so are your potential customers.

We rebuild websites for this new reality. That means:
- **Strategy-first discovery.** Understanding how AI systems perceive and represent your brand. Running a competitive analysis through AI eyes.
- **Content architecture for AI.** Restructuring how your brand story is told so AI systems extract the right narrative and represent you accurately.
- **Technical excellence.** Building on Webflow (or migrating to it) so your site is fast, indexable, and future-proof.
- **Performance-obsessed.** Every decision is measured. Does it move the needle on traffic, conversion, brand perception?

We don't do web redesigns. We do web transformations. The outcome is a website that works harder as a growth asset — better discoverability, better representation, better conversion.

### Part 3: Why Now

Three converging forces have made this the critical moment:

**Force 1: AI-Era Discoverability**
Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the new SEO. LLMs don't just find your website — they synthesize it and present it as fact to users. If your website isn't optimized for how AI reads and understands you, you're losing control of your narrative. This isn't a 2026 problem. It's a now problem.

**Force 2: Website Structure is Outdated**
Most B2B SaaS websites were built for human readers and Google bots — optimized for keyword rankings, page load speed, and conversion funnels. The structure, information architecture, and content patterns that ranked well in Google don't work for AI systems. They need different signals: clearer entity relationships, more comprehensive brand definitions, structured reasoning.

**Force 3: New Marketing Leadership = Pressure for Early Wins**
80% of our engagements start with a new CMO, VP Marketing, or Head of Growth who's just landed. These leaders are under immediate pressure to show impact. They can't wait 12 months for a full brand overhaul or a sales tool rebuild. The website is the fastest, lowest-risk lever to demonstrate leadership, credibility, and forward-thinking strategy.

The window is narrow. CMOs with outdated websites will lose mindshare to competitors who've modernized. The companies that move now will own the narrative in how AI represents them.

### Part 4: Proof Points

**Data from 42 Target Accounts (All B2B SaaS, All on Webflow):**
- **Performance Score Average: 63.2/100.** Significant room for improvement across the board. This is not a nice-to-have problem — these sites are leaving money on the table.
- **AI Brand Report Data:** Companies running their sites through our AI Brand Tool (comparison of self-description vs. how ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini describe them) are seeing scores in the 50-70 range. Simple fixes yield 15-25 point improvements.
- **Job Change Signals:** 100% of Tier 1 accounts have leadership changes (new CMO/VP Marketing) in the past 12 months. New leaders = budget, motivation, and urgency.
- **Traffic Scale:** Target accounts range from 55K to 4.5M monthly traffic. We've worked at every scale. Our Tier 1 accounts include Hiya (4.5M), FlutterFlow (1.4M), Workiz (796K), EventsAir (674K), and others. We know how to move the needle at enterprise volume.

**Teaching Events as Proof:**
When we host teaching events, attendees experience the aha moment themselves. They see the problem, understand the fix, realize the complexity, and decide to hire. This isn't a webinar pitch — it's a hands-on workshop where they leave knowing what they'd need to do internally vs. where they need expert help. That realization sells itself.

**Network Depth:**
VAN's full service suite means we're not a single-track vendor. We can go deep on technical execution, or shift to brand strategy, or tackle performance marketing. That flexibility is a moat. It means we don't oversell the wrong service and we can evolve the engagement as needs emerge.

### Part 5: How Engagements Work

Engagements follow a predictable model: **Pilot → Retainer → Expansion.**

**Pilot (Weeks 1-6, ~$25K-$50K):**
A focused, scoped sprint to validate the approach and deliver visible quick wins. Example: AI Brand Optimization sprint — we audit how AI systems perceive the company, deliver a prioritized fix roadmap, and implement top 3-5 high-impact changes. Pilot proves ROI and scope-sets the bigger engagement.

**Retainer (Months 3+, $10K-$25K/month, typically 6-12 months):**
Ongoing strategic partnership to execute the transformation roadmap. This is where the real work happens: content architecture overhaul, technical migration, performance optimization, ongoing AEO tuning. The retainer is the vehicle for the client to access the full network when they need it — a strategic partner, not a task vendor.

**Expansion (Months 6+):**
As initial wins compound, scope expands. What started as website optimization becomes broader: brand refresh, demand generation, SEO content strategy, product marketing. The first win opens the door to the second, third, fourth.

Why this model works:
- **Low risk entry.** Pilot is small enough to slip into existing budget. Low commitment, high proof.
- **Fast credibility.** Quick wins in the first 4-6 weeks build internal champions. Makes it easy to approve retainer.
- **Predictable value delivery.** Retainer turns into a trusted partnership. The client knows who to call, the team knows the brand, momentum compounds.

## SECTION 7: USAGE GUIDE

### When to Use Tier 1 vs. Tier 2 Templates

**Use Tier 1 (1:1 ABM) for:**
- Accounts with new leadership (CMO, VP Marketing, Head of Growth within last 12 months)
- Accounts with traffic >500K/month (higher revenue potential)
- Accounts with confirmed product-market fit and strong growth trajectory
- High-engagement signals in target industries (funding announcements, expansions, new hires)
- Accounts where you have a warm introduction or strong reference potential

**Use Tier 2 (1:Few ABM) for:**
- Accounts with good fit but no recent leadership change
- Accounts with 100K-500K/month traffic
- Accounts in targeted segments but slightly lower engagement probability
- Accounts where you want to test product-market fit before deeper investment
- Accounts that can be efficiently grouped by segment or industry vertical

**Decision Framework:**
If you're going to invest 5+ hours on research, personalization, and custom touches, use Tier 1. If you're using segment templates and slight customization per company, use Tier 2. The line between them is: How much unique research are you doing per account?

### Do's and Don'ts for VAN Outreach

#### DO's:

1. **Lead with teaching and value.** Every first touch should offer something (report, insight, workshop), never ask for a meeting.
2. **Reference data.** Use the AI Brand Report scores, the segment benchmarks, the account research. Real data beats generic messaging.
3. **Name the problem specifically.** Not "Your website could be better." Instead: "AI systems are picking up your pricing as your primary message, but your website leads with product capabilities."
4. **Acknowledge alternatives.** Show you're not desperate to close. "You could definitely build this capability in-house, or you could bring in expert help. Either way..."
5. **Keep it conversational.** This is peer-to-peer communication. Write like you'd talk to a colleague, not a prospect.
6. **Respect their time.** Short emails (under 150 words). Short LinkedIn messages (under 100). One clear ask per message.
7. **Personalize the placeholders.** Don't send templated messages with [PLACEHOLDERS] unfilled. Fill them in with real data.
8. **Track engagement.** Note who opened, who clicked, who responded. Use that signal to inform the next touch.
9. **Escalate to Yaya for warm handoffs.** If a Tier 1 contact responds positively, Yaya should own the first executive conversation.
10. **Use multiple channels.** Email + LinkedIn together is more effective than either alone. Vary it to avoid spam filter fatigue.

#### DON'Ts:

1. **Don't ask for a meeting in the first touch.** "Let's hop on a call" kills the teaching-first posture. Ask for feedback, not time.
2. **Don't use corporate buzzwords.** Avoid: "synergy," "leverage," "alignment," "stakeholder," "moving the needle" (wait, we use this one — OK for VAN internal, not for outreach).
3. **Don't mention price in outreach.** Let them ask. You set expectations in the first conversation, not the first email.
4. **Don't send generic content.** "I thought you'd like this article" without context. Always frame why it's relevant to THEM.
5. **Don't follow up if they engage elsewhere.** If they respond positively, drop the sequence and move to conversation. Don't send scheduled follow-ups on top of active dialogue.
6. **Don't oversell VAN's breadth.** Don't say "We do strategy, design, content, dev, SEO, paid media..." Use specificity: "We specialize in website-as-growth-system modernization."
7. **Don't wait longer than 24 hours to respond if they engage.** Responsiveness signals maturity and respect.
8. **Don't send personal messages from automated tools.** Write them by hand (or make them look hand-written). A cold email that reads templated gets worse response.
9. **Don't reference competitors negatively.** Don't say, "Other agencies miss X." Just explain what we do differently.
10. **Don't give up after 4 touches.** Some people need more time. Deprioritize, but re-engage if a trigger event happens (funding, acquisition, new hire).

### Integration with the Content Engine

Teaching events are the linchpin of the sales motion. They're where teaching-first becomes selling-without-selling.

3. **Conversation starter.** Content gives them something to react to that's not "Do you want to hire us?" They can engage with ideas first, relationship second.
4. **Social proof.** Content that gets shared, commented on, and cited builds VAN's profile. Showing that profile in outreach matters.

### How to Handle Responses

#### Positive Response (Attended workshop or asked to learn more):

**Your Job:** Transition from teaching to sales conversation. Move to Yaya for Tier 1 accounts. For Tier 2, you can own the initial sales call.

**Within 24 hours:**
1. Thank them for engaging
2. Suggest a specific next step: "I'd like to dig into your specific situation. How about 20 minutes Thursday at 2pm?" (Give a concrete time, not "let me know when you're free")
3. Send a calendar invite with an agenda (shows professionalism)

**Agenda for that call:**
- Their current situation and challenges (listen more than talk)
- Specific gaps from the AI Brand Report
- What a pilot engagement would look like
- Timeline and investment range

**Rule:** You're moving from "teaching" to "discovery" but with the same principle: Ask questions, listen, only recommend action when they ask.

#### Neutral Response (Opened email, didn't respond; viewed content; mild interest signal):

**Your Job:** Provide more value and create lower-friction engagement

**Your move:**
1. Send the next templated message on schedule
2. If pattern continues (3-4 touches with low engagement), stop active outreach but add them to monthly educational newsletter
3. Re-trigger only if: job change event, funding announcement, industry conference, or new content launch that directly addresses their challenge

#### Negative Response (Said "no" or "not interested now"):

**Your Job:** Leave the door open, don't push back

**Your move:**
1. Respond with grace: "Totally understand. If that changes, you know where to find me. Enjoy the build!"
